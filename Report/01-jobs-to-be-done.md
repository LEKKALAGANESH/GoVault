# Jobs To Be Done Analysis

## Product Vision
**GoVault** — The post-booking travel companion that turns scattered confirmations into a beautiful, organized journey tracker.

---

## Market Context

### The Booking-to-Travel Gap
Most travel tools focus on **discovery and planning** (where to go, what to book). Very few serve the critical phase **after booking but before/during travel** — when travelers need to:
- Organize scattered confirmations
- Share info with travel companions
- Access everything offline
- Track spending in real-time
- Have emergency info at fingertips

### Competitive Landscape

| Tool | Focus | Gap |
|------|-------|-----|
| TripIt | Business travel, email parsing | Utilitarian UI, no expense tracking |
| Wanderlog | Collaborative planning | Weak post-booking, cluttered |
| Google Trips | Simple organization | Discontinued |
| Notion/Sheets | Flexible but manual | No travel-specific features |
| Splitwise | Expense splitting only | No itinerary context |

**Opportunity**: A beautiful, offline-first trip tracker focused on the **execution phase** of travel.

---

## Primary User Personas

### 1. The Family Trip Organizer (Primary)
**Profile**: 30-45, organizing trips for multi-generational family (parents, kids, elderly)
**Pain**: Juggling everyone's needs, sharing info across tech comfort levels, keeping everyone coordinated
**Quote**: "I have 47 emails with booking confirmations and my mom keeps asking me what time the flight is"

### 2. The Frequent Leisure Traveler
**Profile**: 25-40, takes 3-5 trips/year, values experiences
**Pain**: Wants to understand spending patterns, create shareable memories
**Quote**: "I never know how much a trip actually cost me until I'm shocked by my credit card statement"

### 3. The Group Trip Coordinator
**Profile**: Any age, organizing trips with friends
**Pain**: Expense splitting, keeping everyone on the same page
**Quote**: "Half the group doesn't know where we're staying tomorrow"

## Jobs To Be Done

### Core Job Statement
> When I've finalized my travel bookings, I want to consolidate everything into one accessible place, so I can travel stress-free without hunting for information.

---

### Job 1: Consolidation & Organization
**When** I've booked flights, hotels, and activities across multiple platforms
**I want to** bring all confirmations into one organized view
**So I can** stop digging through emails and have everything at my fingertips

**Struggling Moments:**
- Searching Gmail for "confirmation" 2 days before trip
- Screenshotting booking details to WhatsApp self-chat
- Printing 15 pages of PDFs "just in case"
- Partner asking "what's the hotel address?" for the 5th time

**Success Criteria:**
- All trip info accessible in <5 seconds
- No need to open email during trip
- Can show confirmation to hotel staff directly from phone

---

### Job 2: Sharing & Coordination
**When** I'm traveling with others (family, friends, colleagues)
**I want to** give everyone access to the same trip information
**So we can** stay coordinated without me being the single point of failure

**Struggling Moments:**
- Forwarding the same email to 4 family members
- Creating a WhatsApp group just for trip logistics
- Mom calling to ask what time to be at the airport
- Group splitting up and someone not knowing the hotel name

**Success Criteria:**
- Share trip with one link/invite
- Everyone sees real-time updates
- Works for non-tech-savvy travelers (elderly parents)

---

### Job 3: Offline Access
**When** I'm traveling internationally or in areas with poor connectivity
**I want to** access all my trip details without internet
**So I can** show confirmations, find addresses, and navigate even offline

**Struggling Moments:**
- Landing in a new country without data, need hotel address
- In a taxi with no signal, can't pull up destination
- Showing immigration the return ticket but email won't load

**Success Criteria:**
- 100% of critical info available offline
- Documents (PDFs, images) cached locally
- Works in airplane mode

---

### Job 4: Real-Time Expense Tracking
**When** I'm spending money during my trip
**I want to** log expenses as they happen with minimal friction
**So I can** stay on budget and know my true trip cost

**Struggling Moments:**
- Forgetting what the cash withdrawals were for
- Currency conversion confusion (how much did I actually spend?)
- End of trip: "Wait, we spent HOW much?"
- Splitting costs with travel partners fairly

**Success Criteria:**
- Log expense in <10 seconds
- Automatic currency conversion
- Running total always visible
- Split tracking with companions
- Post-trip expense breakdown by category

---

### Job 5: Emergency Preparedness
**When** something goes wrong during travel (medical, lost documents, missed connection)
**I want to** have all emergency information immediately accessible
**So I can** handle the situation without panic

**Struggling Moments:**
- Kid gets sick — where's the nearest hospital?
- Lost passport — what's the embassy number?
- Missed flight — what was the airline's rebooking number?
- Family back home needs to reach us urgently

**Success Criteria:**
- Emergency contacts one tap away
- Local emergency numbers pre-loaded for destination
- Travel insurance details accessible
- SOS sharing with home contacts

---

### Job 6: Trip Memory & Insights
**When** I return from a trip
**I want to** have a record of what I did and spent
**So I can** reminisce, share highlights, and budget better for future trips

**Struggling Moments:**
- "What was that amazing restaurant called?"
- Trying to remember trip costs for next year's planning
- Wanting to share itinerary with friends planning similar trip

**Success Criteria:**
- Searchable history of past trips
- Expense insights and patterns
- Shareable trip summary
- Photo memories attached to days/places

## Forces Analysis

### Push (Away from current solutions)
- Email search is painful and slow
- Spreadsheets require too much manual work
- TripIt feels like corporate software
- WhatsApp groups get cluttered
- Can't track expenses in context of itinerary

### Pull (Toward new solution)
- One beautiful interface for everything
- Works offline reliably
- Family can access without learning new tool
- Expenses tracked alongside activities
- Feels personal, not corporate

### Anxiety (About switching)
- "What if it doesn't have my confirmation when I need it?"
- "Will my family actually use this?"
- "Is my data safe?"
- "What happens if the app company shuts down?"

### Habit (Current behavior)
- Starring emails in Gmail
- Screenshot to WhatsApp
- Printing PDFs
- Mental math for expenses
- Shared Google Doc/Sheet

## Business Opportunity

### Revenue Model Options

**1. Freemium**
- Free: 1 active trip, basic features
- Pro ($4.99/trip or $29/year): Unlimited trips, expense tracking, document storage, sharing

**2. B2B White-Label**
- Travel agencies create branded trip trackers for clients
- Tour operators provide as value-add
- Corporate travel management

**3. Affiliate & Partnerships**
- Travel insurance upsell
- Currency exchange partners
- Last-minute booking links
- Local experience recommendations

### Key Metrics
- Trips created per user
- Documents uploaded per trip
- Expenses logged per trip
- Share rate (trips shared with others)
- Return rate (users creating 2nd trip)

### Moat Potential
- Network effects from sharing (recipient sees value, creates own trips)
- Data moat (expense history, travel patterns)
- Switching cost (document vault, trip history)
